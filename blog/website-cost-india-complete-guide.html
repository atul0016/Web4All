How Much Does a Website Cost in India? Complete 2026 Guide

By SA-Flow • April 12, 2026 • 6 min read

If you've ever asked "how much does a website cost?" — you know the answer is frustratingly vague. That's because website pricing in India ranges from ₹1,500 to ₹5,00,000 and beyond. This guide breaks down exactly what you get at every price point, so you can make an informed decision.

Website Cost Breakdown: India 2026

Here's a realistic overview of what different budgets get you in the Indian market:

Budget Range What You Get Best For
₹1,500 – ₹5,000 Single-page or multi-page static website, mobile responsive, basic SEO, contact form Local shops, freelancers, new businesses
₹5,000 – ₹25,000 Custom design, CMS (WordPress), blog, image gallery, WhatsApp integration, Google Analytics Growing businesses, restaurants, clinics
₹25,000 – ₹1,00,000 E-commerce with payment gateway, inventory management, admin dashboard, advanced SEO Online stores, mid-size businesses
₹1,00,000 – ₹5,00,000+ Custom web application, API integrations, user authentication, real-time features, SaaS platform Enterprises, startups, SaaS companies

What Factors Affect Website Cost?

1. Number of Pages

A single-page website is obviously cheaper than a 20-page website. Most small businesses in India need only 1-5 pages: Home, About, Services/Products, Gallery, and Contact. Don't pay for pages you don't need.

2. Custom Design vs Templates

A template-based website costs ₹1,500-₹10,000. A fully custom design from scratch costs ₹25,000+. The truth? For most local businesses, a well-customized template looks just as professional as a custom design.

3. Features & Functionality

A simple informational website costs much less than one with online ordering, appointment booking, or e-commerce. Be honest about what features you actually need right now — you can always add more later.

4. CMS (Content Management System)

If you want to update your website yourself, you'll need a CMS like WordPress. This adds complexity and cost (₹5,000-₹15,000 extra), plus ongoing hosting costs. If your content doesn't change often, a static website is cheaper and faster.

5. Ongoing Costs

Don't forget the recurring expenses:

  • Domain name: ₹500 – ₹1,200/year (.com, .in)
  • Hosting: ₹1,000 – ₹5,000/year (shared hosting) or free with GitHub Pages
  • SSL certificate: Free with most hosts (Let's Encrypt)
  • Maintenance: ₹2,000 – ₹5,000/year (optional)

Where to Get a Website Built in India

Freelancer Platforms (₹2,000 – ₹30,000)

Websites like Fiverr, Upwork, and Freelancer have Indian developers offering websites from ₹2,000. Quality varies wildly. You might get a great site, or a WordPress template with your logo slapped on.

Web Design Agencies (₹25,000 – ₹5,00,000)

Agencies in cities like Bangalore, Pune, and Delhi charge ₹50,000+ for basic websites. You get project management, multiple revisions, and ongoing support. But for a small shop, this is overkill.

Local Web Designers (₹1,500 – ₹25,000)

This is often the sweet spot for small businesses. A local designer who understands your market, speaks your language, and delivers quickly. At SA-Flow, we specialize in exactly this — professional websites starting at ₹1,500 for businesses across 38+ categories.

How to Avoid Overpaying

  1. Know your requirements — List the pages and features you actually need before contacting anyone.
  2. Get 3 quotes — Compare pricing from at least 3 providers. If someone is 10x more expensive, ask why.
  3. Check their portfolio — A good designer will show you live websites they've built. Visit them on mobile.
  4. Ask about ongoing costs — Make sure you understand domain, hosting, and maintenance charges upfront.
  5. Start small, grow later — Get a basic website live now. Add features as your business grows and budget allows.

The Bottom Line

For most small businesses in India — grocery stores, salons, boutiques, pharmacies — a website between ₹1,500 and ₹5,000 is all you need to establish a professional online presence. Don't let anyone convince you to spend ₹50,000 on a WordPress site when a well-designed static website does the job better, faster, and cheaper.
